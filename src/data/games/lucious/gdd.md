# 1. Game Overview
## 1.1 High-Concept Pitch
Lucious is a single-player, open-world action-adventure RPG set in a richly imagined world ruled by five immortal beings known as the Reapers. The player controls Lucious, a young man orphaned at fifteen, who travels the world, assembles an unlikely team, and stumbles into the center of a centuries-old prophecy — one that may see him overthrow the most powerful rulers the world has ever known and restore what was stolen from its people.

## 1.3 Elevator Pitch
*"He had nothing. He inherited everything. Now the world wants it back — and the Reapers want him dead."*

A fifteen-year-old boy loses his parents and inherits his father's dual pistols and his wits. Four years of wandering the world — surviving jungles, learning to fight on a fishing boat, mastering hand-to-hand combat on a mysterious ancient island — forge him into someone extraordinary. When he reaches the city of Kwai and discovers a dead uncle he never knew, he also discovers a secret: his uncle was building the world's largest vessel, funding a conspiracy theorist's world-changing project, and quietly preparing for a revolution against the five immortal Reapers who govern every nation. Lucious didn't come looking for a destiny. But one came looking for him.

## 1.4 Design Pillars
| | |
|---|---|
| **PILLAR 1: A World Worth Exploring** | Every region of this world is distinct, beautiful, and alive with culture, secrets, and gameplay opportunity. Travel should feel like discovery — every biome, city, and island has something unique to offer. |
| **PILLAR 2: An Ensemble That Feels Real** | Lucious does not save the world alone. The ensemble cast — Spunky, Stone, Naphteli, Naki, Vamos, Dragos — each brings personality, combat capability, and emotional stakes. The team dynamic is as important as the action. |
| **PILLAR 3: Freedom of Approach** | Whether on foot, on water, or under the ocean, the player should feel free to approach objectives their way. Drive the vehicle, delegate to a teammate, or skip the journey entirely — the game respects the player's time and style. |
| **PILLAR 4: Lore Hidden in Plain Sight** | The world's deepest secrets — the truth about the Reapers, the Ne-aym people, the Areis civilization under the ice — are discovered organically through exploration, conversation, and side quests, not exposition dumps. |

# 2. World & Setting
## 2.1 Overview
The game takes place in a large, diverse world divided into distinct geographic and cultural regions. The world is controlled by a World Government, headed by five immortal rulers called the Reapers, who enforce their rule through a paramilitary police force called the Enforcers. The world has a complex hidden history — one the Reapers have deliberately erased and rewritten — which drives the game's central mystery and narrative.

## 2.2 World Regions
| | |
|---|---|
| **The Imperial City** | The fortified capital of the world. Surrounded by four wide rivers, considered un-attackable. Accessible only by train as no aircraft yet exist. Home to aristocrats, intellectuals, and the five Reapers themselves. The ultimate destination — and the final obstacle. |
| **Kwai (The Port City)** | An industrial city of factories and sprawling ghettos. Most people come here for work before hoping to move to the Imperial City, though low wages and a thriving drug scene trap most. Lucious's story begins here upon discovering his uncle's estate and Dr. Wiess's workshop on nearby Death Island. |
| **Death Island** | An island off the coast of Kwai. Despite its name, it is the location of Dr. Wiess's massive secret workshop — a laboratory and drydock three floors underground, surrounded by a crater of mountains and fed by a wide river. Home base of operations for most of the game. |
| **The Waishu Mountains** | Resembling ancient Japan in culture and aesthetic. No electricity or advanced technology. The people possess deep knowledge of the ancient world and keep its true history. Home to Raiko, keeper of the prophecy. |
| **The Island of Hoola** | Resembling Hawaii in people and landscape. Nice, respectable people willing to help. They export food and clothing to the Port City to support their economy. |
| **The Great Jungle** | South of the Port City — thick, lush, resembling South American rainforest. Largely unexplored. Hidden Aboriginal peoples still live within it. Home to the Stoka flower and the street drug Vonk. |
| **The Highlands** | Resembling Ireland in people and culture. Have been quietly forming a militia and planning a revolution for years. |
| **Zafar Veras** | A Las Vegas-style city where gambling, entertainment, and vice are the local economy. Named from the Spanish phrase meaning 'Escape Reality.' |
| **Tifon** | An old city with German/Russian-style architecture. The original home of the Reapers. Humans have since moved in. |
| **The Land of Mo** | An ancient land believed to be where mankind originated. The first written history was found here. Lucious spends years here learning hand-to-hand combat. |
| **The Ne-aym Prison** | Located near the Southern Ice Cap. A fortified prison where captured Ne-aym people are held. Five are brought to the Imperial City annually as a show of dominance. |
| **The Land of Ne-aym** | Considered a myth by most. The origin point of all magic. Now uninhabitable after the Reaper war. The Waishu people know the truth. |
| **The City of Ereas** | An underwater city hidden beneath the Southern Ice Cap. Home of the Areis people — ancient shapeshifters from a dying planet. Anchored by four massive cables to the ocean floor. |

## 2.3 The World's Hidden History
- Humans once ruled the world through a bloodline of kings. The Reapers emerged roughly one thousand years ago — originally peaceful, turned violent when they discovered they could control the elements.
- The Reapers absorb the knowledge and skills of whoever they kill — their most terrifying ability, and the source of their name.
- A Reaper named Kaze trespassed on the land of Ne-aym and discovered that anyone can harness magical power. He became hateful and power-hungry, manipulating his race. That is when the Reapers turned.
- The Reapers destroyed the human king, built the Enforcers army, and took control. Once thousands, they began dying mysteriously until only five remain publicly.
- The Ne-aym people — golden-haired, blue-eyed, dark-skinned — were the original keepers of magic. Driven from their land, they hide across the world and are still hunted today.
- The Areis, hidden beneath the ice cap, have observed everything. They have quietly preserved eight Reapers to prevent total extinction of the race.

## 3.2 Protector (Dog Companion)
A big black dog resembling a Rottweiler, given to Lucious at age eight. Named after saving Lucious's life during an Enforcer incident. His constant companion through four years of travel.

Late game revelation: Protector is an Areis — one of the shapeshifting people from the underwater city of Ereas. Placed with Lucious's family specifically to protect him. When the Areis elders greet him in Ereas, Protector transforms into his true form before the team. He chooses to remain with Lucious in dog form.

# 4. Story Overview
## 4.1 Backstory — Before the Game
Lucious received Protector as a puppy at age eight. At fifteen, he learned his parents were dead. He packed his father's dual pistols and left. Four years of travel followed:

- Hiked the Great Jungle to the coastal city of Cidra. Hitched a fishing boat — six months at sea teaching him navigation and cards.
- Landed on the Land of Mo. Lived two and a half years with the Aborigines, learning hand-to-hand combat.
- Traveled to the Island of Hoola, then on to Kwai.

## 4.2 Act 1 — Kwai and Death Island
Arriving in Kwai, Lucious discovers a man with his same last name just died. A DNA test confirms the connection. First player control: walking through an open-air market when Spunky steals his money pouch. The chase is the movement tutorial — sprinting and leaping across rooftops. Spunky is caught later, talks his way onto the team, and tells Lucious everything about Uncle Tiberius and Death Island.

Lucious goes to Death Island, meets Dr. Wiess and the Drages, sees the massive boat under construction, gets his own speedboat (vehicle tutorial), and learns what three parts are still needed to complete the great vessel.

## 4.3 Act 2 — Gathering the Parts
- TIFON: Lucious retrieves the first part. Meets Vamos — suspicious, doesn't join yet.
- TRAIN TO BONN: Active train journey with side activities. In Bonn, Lucious saves Naphteli from Enforcers. He and Stone end up in jail. Spunky breaks them out.
- THE DESERT: Spunky and Dragos retrieve the second part. The third part requires fighting a massive arachnid in a desert cave. All parts secured. Stone joins. The boat nears completion.

## 4.4 Act 3 — The World Opens
- SPUNKY'S MISSION: Goes to Zafar Veras for prison intelligence. Returns with Naki and a birth certificate he found in a stolen briefcase — naming Vamos as Kayne's father. He keeps it secret for now.
- LUCIOUS AND STONE: Drive to the Waishu Pass, hike through monsters, reach Raiko. Meet Vamos fighting a monster alone and help him. Raiko reveals the prophecy. Naki joins. Vamos joins after a specific mountain event. Full team assembled on Death Island.

## 4.5 Act 4 — The Ne-aym Prison Rescue
The great boat is complete. Team tours it, Dragos trains Lucious to operate it. Attack plan: Dr. Wiess's submarine slips under the barrier undetected. Lucious pilots it to the prison island. The team lands, destroys the electricity source (killing the Mage Field suppressing Ne-aym magic), destroys guard towers, and rescues 376 Ne-aym prisoners. Naphteli is found — she hugs Stone, then Lucious. Everyone boards the great vessel and returns to Death Island.

## 4.6 Act 5 — The Underwater Discovery
Dr. Wiess suggests exploring the Southern Ice Cap by submarine. The team navigates underwater ice tunnels into a vast space where four massive cables anchor to the ocean floor, surrounding a city. They are pulled into a protective bubble — the City of Ereas.

Two Areis dignitaries emerge. One approaches Protector: 'It's been many years, my brother.' Protector transforms into his true Areis form. Lucious is speechless. The Areis explain their origin and that Protector was placed specifically to protect Lucious. Vamos is shown a hidden section containing about twenty living Reapers preserved by the Areis. He declines to meet them — 'Not right now.'

The team receives Areis underwater breathing equipment and the Psiedon summoning device. Underwater exploration and combat unlock across the entire world.

## 4.7 Act 6 — The Land of Ne-aym
The team slips under the barrier by submarine to reach the ruined Land of Ne-aym. Struggling through the haunted ruins, they reach the ancient temple and solve three puzzles to place the sacred orb. A shockwave pulses outward — collapsing the Reaper barrier entirely. The spirits are laid to rest. An ancient Ne-aym spirit channels through a willing host and tells them: everyone can now use magic. Bring my people home.

The 376 rescued Ne-ayms return to rebuild their land. Dr. Wiess sends Drages to help.

## 4.8 The Celebration and Beyond
Back on Death Island: bonfire, food, dancing, Drages playing music. Spunky confronts Vamos with the birth certificate. Vamos rips it up and throws it in the fire. Dr. Wiess says 'Of course, haha!' and walks away. The truth is known. Nothing is said.

The next morning Dr. Wiess gives Lucious a list of supplies for the next phase. The open world is now fully available for side quests, weapon hunts, summon quests, and underwater exploration — while the path to the Imperial City and the final confrontation with the Reapers lies ahead.

# 5. Gameplay & Mechanics
## 5.1 Core Gameplay Loop
| | |
|---|---|
| **Moment-to-Moment** | Combat, exploration, and conversation across diverse environments. |
| **Session-Level** | Complete story missions and side quests to advance the plot, grow the team, and unlock new areas and vehicles. |
| **Long-Term** | Build the team and base toward the assault on the Imperial City. The world grows more open with each Act. |

## 5.2 Movement & Traversal
- URBAN: Free-running through cities. The Spunky chase in Kwai is the movement tutorial.
- GROUND VEHICLES: Compact vehicle. Lucious drives or delegates to a companion. Long journeys can be skipped.
- WATER: Personal speedboat for Lucious. The great vessel for major operations.
- TRAIN: Certain journeys use trains with activities available during the ride.
- SUBMARINE: Unlocked in Act 5. Lucious pilots it. Opens underwater exploration and combat.
- THE GREAT VESSEL: The game's largest vehicle. 12 cannons per side, deployable rear dock for the speedboat.

## 5.3 Combat — Character Roster
| | |
|---|---|
| **Lucious** | Dual pistols + hand-to-hand combat. Fast and adaptable. |
| **Spunky** | Bola (restraint/disruption). Stealth-focused. Knows patrol patterns. |
| **Stone** | Shotgun with array of ammo. Giant fists for melee. Crowd control. |
| **Naphteli** | Ne-aym magic (wave-based energy) + dagger. Magic fully unlocked after prison. |
| **Naki** | Staff combat + Waishu magic. Wave-of-destruction area damage. |
| **Vamos** | Broadsword + Reaper elemental abilities (lightning, fire, ice). Most powerful fighter. Used selectively. |
| **Dragos** | Logistics and vehicle-mounted weapons. Not a frontline fighter. |

## 5.4 Character Switching
Multi-part missions use deliberate character switching — for example, the player plays as Spunky in Zafar Veras before switching back to Lucious. This is narrative-driven, not a seamless open-world mechanic.

## 5.5 Monster Combat
Wilderness areas — jungle, desert, mountains, underwater — contain monsters. Large monster fights are boss-style encounters with unique mechanics. Key fights: the giant arachnid in the desert cave (boat part) and the ruins battles in the Land of Ne-aym.

## 5.6 The Arena
Dr. Wiess's monster combat arena on Death Island allows character training and upgrades before the big missions. All attacks are physical and thrown at this stage — magic unlocks later.

## 5.7 Side Quests & Open World
- Weapon acquisition quests across all regions
- Summon quests — Psiedon is first, others available through the world
- Underwater combat and exploration (Areis breathing equipment required)
- Small creature battles yielding sellable items
- Gambling and card games in Veras and on trains
- Regional story threads: Highlands militia, Tifon settlers, and others

## 5.8 The Vonk Economy
The drug Vonk, derived from the Stoka flower in the Great Jungle, exists throughout the world. Its medicinal form (Stokanalus) is legal; the street form is a major smuggling economy touching the Port City underground, the Jungle communities, and the Veras criminal networks.

# 6. Races, Magic & The Supernatural
## 6.1 The Five Races
| | |
|---|---|
| **Humans** | The original rulers. Now governed subjects. Most are unaware of true history. |
| **Reapers** | Pale, long black hair (turns white at 1,000 years). Absorb skills from kills. Control elements and summon creatures. Five remain publicly; eight more hidden by the Areis. |
| **Ne-aym** | Golden glowing hair, bright blue eyes, dark skin. Original magic people. Hunted to near-extinction. 376 rescued in Act 4. Land restored in Act 6. |
| **Areis** | Shapeshifters from another world. Blue scales, webbed hands and feet, large round eyes, no nose, gills on neck — true form. Hidden in the City of Ereas for thousands of years. Quietly intervening. Gave Lucious a dog. |
| **Drages** | Small humanoid working race. Must work or wither and die. Enormously strong. Employed humanely by Dr. Wiess. |

## 6.2 Magic System
- Ne-aym magic: Wave-based energy attacks. Suppressed by the Mage Field. Fully unlocked after the prison is destroyed.
- Reaper abilities: Elemental control (lightning, fire, ice), creature summoning, and skill absorption from kills. Vamos uses selectively.
- Waishu magic: Naki's staff-based area destruction.
- Areis gifts: Underwater breathing equipment, Psiedon summoning device, general underwater capability.
- Universal magic: After the sacred orb is placed in the Land of Ne-aym, the ancient spirit announces everyone can now use magic — a significant late-game power unlock for all characters.

## 6.3 The Summons System
Summons are powerful entities called upon in combat. Psiedon is the first (given by the Areis). Additional summons are hidden across the world as side quest rewards.

# 7. Art Direction & Audio
## 7.1 Visual Tone
The world of Lucious should feel diverse, vibrant, and culturally distinct across its regions. The overall aesthetic sits between the vivid cultural diversity of Final Fantasy XV and the grounded action-adventure feel of Uncharted. Technology is pre-aviation throughout.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Kwai / Port City** | Industrial grays and factory browns. Dense and crowded. Neon and dock lighting at night. |
| **Waishu Mountains** | Ancient Japan. Misty, serene, wooden architecture, lanterns, cherry blossoms. |
| **The Great Jungle** | Lush, towering, vivid green. Stoka flowers provide pops of yellow. Aboriginal camps hidden and carefully lit. |
| **Zafar Veras** | Neon-soaked. The world's most artificially bright place. Las Vegas excess at every corner. |
| **Death Island** | Unremarkable exterior. Below ground: dramatic industrial scale — the vessel suspended over black water. |
| **The City of Ereas** | Bioluminescent. Lit entirely by life — the Areis and their technology glow softly blue and green. |
| **Land of Ne-aym** | Ruins and restoration. Initially grey, broken, haunted. After the orb: color returns as spirits are released. |

## 7.2 Character Visual Identity
Each character communicates their story visually: Lucious is lean and worn — the traveler. Stone is enormous and immovable — the mountain. Spunky is always in motion. Naphteli is covered and careful until she doesn't need to be. Vamos looks like a man carrying centuries of weight — because he is.

## 7.3 Audio Direction
Music adapts to each region's cultural identity — traditional Japanese instrumentation in the Waishu Mountains, jazz and big-band in Zafar Veras, industrial percussion in the Port City. The Land of Ne-aym before restoration is near-silent: wind and whispered spirits. After the orb, the land's natural soundtrack grows from nothing into something full and alive.

The great vessel has its own sonic identity — armor plating creaking, engines thrumming, distant ocean. The submarine is near-silent: water pressure and ticking systems.

# 8. Market Positioning & Comparable Titles
## 8.1 Comparable Titles
| | |
|---|---|
| **Final Fantasy XV** | Ensemble cast road-trip adventure, open world, vehicle travel. Lucious matches this in tone and team-travel energy. |
| **Uncharted Series** | Action-adventure, charismatic protagonist, globetrotting diverse environments, humor balanced with high stakes. |
| **One Piece (game adaptations)** | Ensemble of different personalities united by a leader with a destiny, traveling a world with distinct cultural regions. |
| **Tales of Series** | Character-driven RPG ensemble, world mythology being uncovered, evolving combat. |
| **Star Ocean / Xenogears** | Worlds with hidden ancient civilizations and civilizations from other worlds. |

## 8.2 Unique Selling Proposition
No existing open-world RPG combines: a richly multi-racial world with distinct cultures per region, a team of genuinely different personalities with personal stakes in the conflict, underwater civilization discovery, and a central mystery about the nature of history itself — all grounded in an action-adventure structure accessible to a broad audience. The ambition: to feel like the best anime you've ever seen, but playable.

# 10. Open Questions & Future Development
## 10.1 Design Questions to Resolve
- Final working title — 'Lucious' is working; a world name or broader title may be developed.
- The final act — the assault on the Imperial City and confrontation with the five Reapers requires its own detailed document.
- Vamos and Kayne's final confrontation — will Vamos kill his son? Is Kayne redeemable?
- The king's bloodline — how is Lucious's connection confirmed, and what does it mean for the ending?
- The eight preserved Reapers in Ereas — what role do they play in the ending or post-game?
- Protector and the Areis — what is the full extent of their involvement in Lucious's life?
- Post-game — with the Reapers defeated and Ne-aym restored, what does the world look like?
- Platform considerations — PS5 and Xbox Series X|S confirmed. PC may be added.
- Multiplayer — is any form of co-op possible with the ensemble cast?
